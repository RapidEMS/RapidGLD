Evaluating Statistics

The data the Simulator has gathered by running is the most valuable part of GLD.
Let us examine what we've learned from our first made map:

  1. Average Junction Waiting Time
    Are the road users evenly spread over the map?
    Than graph this can be all you need. Important is the average time (Y-axis) and wet her the graph declines (improves/learns).
  2. Total Road Users Arrived
    Should be linear from a certain beginning period in which more first none arrive because all have started, and then a lot arrive because they did not have to wait. Learning and genetical algorithms may do bad in the beginning.
  3. Average Trip Waiting Time
    Depends very much on how many junctions with traffic lights there are compared to the number of road users. Even if an algorithm learns, this may not decrease, as waiting queues will empty into the city.
  4. Total Waiting Queue Length
    Useful to watch for the above.
  5. The statistics window Here you can view all the data in tables or graphs.

More in-depth understanding of a map's effectiveness is better gathered with a large and well thought-out map, where the resulting data averages and traffic flow are realistic and meaningful.
